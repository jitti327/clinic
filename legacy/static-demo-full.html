How we help

Start from where it hurts—then we build a precise plan.

Whether pain is new or years old, we focus on understanding the story, examining movement, and using ultrasound when it changes what we do next.

Sciatica & nerve-related leg pain

Thorough consultation, clear explanation, and imaging when it helps clarify nerve involvement—so decisions feel considered, not rushed.

Neck & upper back strain

Assessment of posture, movement and tissue response—ultrasound used to support diagnosis when symptoms suggest deeper joint or soft-tissue drivers.

Knee & hip osteoarthritis flare-ups

Options such as hyaluronic acid or cortisone, discussed in context of your scans, function and goals—including ultrasound-guided placement when appropriate.

Shoulder pain & impingement patterns

From calcific change to rotator cuff overload—we combine manual assessment with ultrasound to align treatment with what your shoulder is actually doing.

Tendon & sports overload

Running, lifting or repetitive work—we look at load, tissue health and whether PRP or guided injection therapy fits your stage of recovery.

Persistent “undiagnosed” MSK pain

If you have bounced between appointments without clarity, we prioritise listening, structured examination, and ultrasound evidence where it breaks the deadlock.

Unsure where you fit? Use the clinic’s pain locator—then book a consultation to interpret findings with Dr Akhtar.

FAQ

Straight answers—before you book.

Many patients self-refer for private MSK care. If you already have scans or letters, bring them— they can speed up understanding, but they are not always required to begin.

Ultrasound helps visualise the target tissue in real time. The clinic’s own messaging reflects research that guided injections can be more accurate than blind placement—supporting comfort and the likelihood of a successful result.

Hyaluronic acid, cortisone injections and platelet-rich plasma (PRP) are highlighted services on the current site. Suitability always depends on assessment, your medical history, and shared decision-making.
